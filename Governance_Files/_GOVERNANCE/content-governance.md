## 1. Purpose & scope
Govern editorial standards, tone, terminology, and **compliance** for every content surface: the 180-post blog, the FAQ, the 36-term glossary, the regulatory news feed, community/UGC, and all product copy. Content is the platform's **largest claim surface** — more words about oil-and-gas economics live in content than anywhere else — so the governing concern is twofold: (a) never cross from education into **advice**, and (b) keep every figure **provenance-true and vintage-labeled**. A single blog sentence that reads like "you should sell" is a liability and a breach of constitution P2.

**In scope:** blog, FAQ, glossary, news, community moderation tone, product copy, downloads/knowledge-center content. **Out of scope:** the SEO mechanics (`seo-governance.md`, `blog-and-seo-content-governance.md`) and the legal text itself (`legal-compliance-and-claims-governance.md`).

## 2. Editorial standards (MUST)
Content is accurate, sourced, Texas-relevant, owner-educational, and advice-free. It uses **canonical glossary wording** (`glossary-terms-reference.md`) for domain terms so a term means the same thing on every page. Readability is calibrated to audience: plain language for mineral owners (who are often non-experts and scam-wary), precise and technical for professionals. Every **decision-adjacent** piece carries: *"Educational, not financial, legal, or tax advice; verify independently."*

## 3. Voice & tone
Transparency / Empowerment / Legacy. Educational, never hype, never directive, never false-urgency. The brand earns trust by **showing its work** (sources, vintage, limitations), not by making bold promises.

## 4. Compliance rules (derived from `Blog.xlsx` content-compliance findings)
| Do NOT publish | Publish instead |
|---|---|
| Tax specifics as guidance ("here's how to avoid royalty taxes") | General education + "consult a tax professional" |
| Lease-vs-sell as a recommendation | Neutral "factors to weigh" + "verify independently" |
| Bullish market framing presented as fact | Attribute/hedge; label any forecast an estimate |
| "Don't sign the first lease" / directive transaction advice | Educational explanation of lease factors; no directive |
| Nationwide-data implication | Texas data scope; US content is educational only |
| Operator steering ("better lease terms with X") | Neutral, factual operator data only (`operator-directory-governance.md`) |

## 5. Sourcing & citation
Factual claims cite the RRC or another authoritative source; modeled figures are labeled estimates with vintage. Statistics in content must be traceable — an unsourced number is treated as a claim and routed for review. Community/UGC is moderated and carries the "not advice" disclaimer.

## 6. Terminology consistency
The 36 glossary terms are canonical (`glossary-terms-reference.md`). A term's definition **must** be identical everywhere it appears; a definition change propagates to every surface that uses it. Methodology terms (Arps decline, allocation, acre/well, EUR, MVestimate) must match the implementation and stay hedged.

## 7. Freshness, refresh & pruning
High-value content is refreshed on cadence; thin or duplicative posts are consolidated or pruned with a 301 redirect; the news feed exposes an as-of date. Stale figures are a provenance defect, not a cosmetic issue.

## 8. Good vs bad examples
| Good | Bad |
|---|---|
| "Royalty income is generally taxed as ordinary income; consult a tax professional for your situation." | "Here's how to legally avoid taxes on your mineral royalties." |
| "Leasing vs. selling: the factors owners commonly weigh." | "You should sell your minerals now." |
| "Production data is sourced from the Texas RRC, as of June 2026." | "Up-to-the-minute nationwide production data." |
| "An HBP lease is held by production — here's what that means." | "Avoid HBP traps the operators don't want you to know about." |

## 9. Editorial QA checklist
☐ Accurate + sourced ☐ Canonical glossary terms ☐ No advice/steering ☐ No guaranteed outcomes ☐ Texas-scope correct ☐ Readability matches audience ☐ Estimates labeled + vintage ☐ "Not advice" where decisions discussed ☐ Stale figures refreshed ☐ Claims routed to legal.

## 10. Anti-patterns
Tax-as-advice; lease/sell directives; bullish-as-fact; nationwide implication; unsourced stats; inconsistent term definitions; operator steering; stale unlabeled figures.
