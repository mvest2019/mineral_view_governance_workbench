## 1. Purpose & scope
Protect the Mineral View **voice, heritage narrative, and claim discipline** across all copy. Brand is a trust asset in a domain full of scams and overpromises; the rules here keep the brand credible and legally safe. Scope: all customer-facing language (site, portal, blog, email, community moderation tone).

## 2. Brand voice
Plain, trustworthy, Texan, educational. **Owner-facing:** warm, jargon-light, empowering. **Professional-facing:** precise, tooling-oriented. Never hype, never advice, never false urgency.

## 3. Heritage narrative (truthful use only — constitution P7)
| Element | Allowed use | Misuse to avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Family-owned; Cochran family | State as fact | Implying a faceless corporation |
| Founder Ryan Cochran | Founder / owner / operator | Inventing titles/quotes |
| 75+ years in Texas oil & gas | Heritage credibility | Implying the *software/data* is 75 years old |
| 6th-generation Texans | Heritage credibility | Inflating generations |
| Values: Transparency, Empowerment, Legacy | Messaging pillars | Repurposing as guarantees |

## 4. Messaging pillars
- **Transparency** — show sources, vintage, and limitations on every figure.
- **Empowerment** — give owners/pros the data to decide for themselves (explicitly *not* advice).
- **Legacy** — long-term Texas family stewardship.

## 5. Audience messaging
Owner: "Know and value what you own." Professional: "Intelligence and tooling at scale." Enterprise: "Portfolio-scale access." A trust line appears on every data surface: *estimates are modeled; verify independently; not advice.*

## 6. Good vs bad examples
| Good | Bad |
|---|---|
| "MVestimate projects six-year earnings from RRC production and a forward price deck — an estimate, not a guarantee." | "See exactly how much your minerals are worth." |
| "Texas mineral data, sourced from the Railroad Commission." | "Nationwide mineral coverage." |
| "Educational content; not financial, legal, or tax advice." | "You should sell now to lock in value." |
| "Six generations of Texas oil-and-gas roots." | "75 years of cutting-edge software." |

## 7. Anti-patterns
Guaranteed-value language; advice/recommendations; nationwide-coverage implication; embellished heritage; unverifiable testimonials; bullish market framing presented as fact; false scarcity in upgrade prompts.

## 8. Community moderation tone
UGC is allowed but moderated: peer posts that give transactional advice ("don't sign the first lease…") stay up only with the "not advice / verify independently" disclaimer and the moderation policy applied (constitution P2).

## 9. Ownership & review
Owner: Content/Brand. Claims → Legal → Ryan. Review quarterly and on any heritage/claim change.

## Deep context (2026-06-30) — voice, and how we talk about data

**Voice.** Clear, trustworthy, plain-language, owner-first. Explain industry concepts without jargon walls; respect that many users are **non-expert mineral owners**. Confident but never hype; the brand's credibility is its product.

**How we talk about data (MUST):**
- **Estimates are estimates (P3):** say "estimated"/"approximate" for EUR, decline, BOE/month, cashflow; never state a modeled number as a fact.
- **Texas scope (P1):** speak to Texas/RRC reality; don't imply nationwide coverage.
- **No overstatement (P2):** describe coverage/accuracy honestly; avoid "guaranteed," "complete," "all your minerals" unless literally true.
- **No advice:** informational, not investment/legal/tax advice (`Compliance_And_Disclaimers.md`).
- **Canonical terms:** use the glossary's terms consistently across UI microcopy, reports, blogs, and AI answers.

**Channel tone:** product microcopy is concise and action-oriented; education is warm and explanatory; sales/CRM outreach is trust-first and **claim-state-aware**; AI-generated narratives match this voice and never invent figures.
