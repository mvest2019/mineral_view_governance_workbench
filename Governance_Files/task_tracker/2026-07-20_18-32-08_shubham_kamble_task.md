The cornerstone milestone of today's 16-hour sprint was the research, structural design, and initial draft creation for **Farmout Agreement**. A farmout agreement is one of the most critical structural contracts in upstream exploration and development, allowing leaseholders to hedge financial risk while promoting un-drilled acreage.

Today's drafting focused on delivering an end-to-end, master-level educational guide that breaks down complex farmout mechanics into clear, accessible concepts for landmen, investors, and lease owners.

- **Structural Core: Farmor vs. Farmee Dynamics:**
  - *Defining the Relationship:* Established the foundational definition of a Farmout Agreement as a contractual arrangement wherein a lease owner (the **Farmor**) agrees to assign an interest in a leasehold to a third party (the **Farmee**), contingent upon the Farmee performing specified drilling, testing, or completion operations at their sole cost and risk.
  - *Commercial Motivation:* Detailed the primary drivers behind farmouts: enabling the Farmor to maintain expiring leases and obtain valuable subsurface geological data without risking capital, while providing the Farmee access to prime prospective acreage without incurring upfront lease acquisition costs.
- **Earning Structures: Produced-Based vs. Option Farmouts:**
  - *Drill-to-Earn (Earning Well) Mechanics:* Outlined the traditional structural framework where the Farmee earns a specified interest in the subject acreage immediately upon drilling, logging, testing, or completing a single initial well to a designated geological depth or target formation.
  - *Option Farmouts and Continuous Drilling:* Mapped out multi-well option structures, explaining how a Farmee can earn an assignment in an initial earning block while acquiring the contractual right (option) to launch continuous drilling programs across adjacent acreage units.
- **Earning Limits: Wellbore-Only vs. Acreage Assignments:**
  - *Wellbore Assignments:* Explained the limited earning structure where the Farmee earns a working interest exclusively in the specific wellbore drilled (and its dedicated spacing unit), leaving the Farmor in full control of surrounding deep rights and step-out locations.
  - *Acreage Assignments:* Detailed broader earning covenants where the Farmee earns an assignment across the entire target block, often subject to acreage depth-severance clauses that retain deep or shallow mineral rights for the Farmor.
- **Financial & Legal Reversionary Rights: BPO, APO, and Back-In Provisions:**
  - *Before Payout (BPO) Allocations:* Documented the cash flow mechanics during the initial recovery phase, where the Farmee typically absorbs 100% of the financial burden and receives 100% (or a major share) of the net revenue interest (minus retained overriding royalties).
  - *Overriding Royalty Interest (ORRI) Conversion:* Explained how Farmors protect their downside by retaining a cost-free Overriding Royalty Interest during the BPO stage.
  - *After Payout (APO) Back-In Option:* Detailed the exact financial trigger when cumulative revenues equal initial drilling and operating expenses. The text explains how the Farmor's retained ORRI converts (backs in) into a cost-bearing Working Interest (e.g., a 25% WI), shifting the well into a standard joint venture operation.
