Question:
What data sources back the "Texas Oil Is Booming" claim, and who fact-checked the figures before publish?

Answer:
The "Texas Oil Is Booming" claim reflects aggregate statewide trends from agencies like the EIA and RRC. This content is an educational guide for mineral owners, not a financial report requiring third-party certification.

The disconnect between statewide headlines and individual checks occurs because:

Statewide figures aggregate thousands of wells, while checks depend on specific mineral interest performance.

New wells elsewhere can boost state output while your specific well declines.

Individual wells face site-specific issues like natural depletion, maintenance, or equipment failure.

Market price changes affect income even if production volumes remain stable.

Lease-specific deductions like transport, taxes, or processing fees directly impact net payments.

Changes in decimal interest, title issues, or accounting corrections alter payouts regardless of production.
