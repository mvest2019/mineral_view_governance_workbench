Which "modern SaaS usability standards" are you measuring against, and is that a documented benchmark or a judgment call?

Answer:
The review is based on established UX and SaaS design principles rather than a single formal benchmark. The recommendations are informed by widely accepted usability practices, including consistency, clear navigation, information hierarchy, discoverability, accessibility, cognitive load reduction, visual hierarchy, conversion-focused user flows, and heuristic evaluation principles (such as Nielsen's usability heuristics). Therefore, references to "modern SaaS usability standards" should be understood as an expert judgment based on these recognized best practices, not as a comparison against a specific certification or documented industry standard.
